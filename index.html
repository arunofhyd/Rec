Why use Rec?

🚀

Native & Fast

Built with Apple's ScreenCaptureKit and AVFoundation. Directly encodes to .mov using hardware acceleration.

🔊

Internal Audio

Seamlessly captures your Mac's internal audio right alongside your video feed. No third-party drivers or loopback extensions required.

🎯

Multiple Modes

Record your entire screen, select a specific region, or focus exclusively on a specific application window.

⚙️

Custom Quality

Adjust your Framerate (30 or 60 FPS), Resolution (Native Retina, 1080p, or 720p), and set the target Video Bitrate.

⏱️

Countdown Timer

Set a 3 or 5-second countdown delay before the recording officially begins, giving you time to prepare your screen.

🪟

Floating Controls

An unobtrusive floating control panel gives you quick access to start and stop recordings and automatically hides from the final video.

Say hello to Rec

Rec is a beautifully simple, lightweight alternative to complex screen recording suites. It gets out of your way and does one thing exceptionally well: native, high-quality screen and audio capture without the fuss.

🔐

Internal Audio without the drivers

Most screen recorders require you to install messy third-party loopback extensions just to capture system audio. Rec uses modern macOS APIs to capture it natively.

🎚️

Capture exactly what you need

Record your entire screen, click and drag to select a specific region, or lock the recording to a specific app window. The choice is yours.

🛡️

Floating, out-of-the-way UI

A sleek floating control panel lets you start, stop, and configure recordings. Best of all, it automatically hides itself from your final video.

🪶

Customizable Quality

Adjust your framerate, set a countdown timer, and choose between Native Retina, 1080p, or 720p scaling. Export directly to a standard .mov file.

Installation

Install via Terminal

  1. Download the installer using the button above.
  2. Open Terminal (⌘+Space, type "Terminal", press Enter).
  3. Type sh — that's s, h, then a space.
  4. Drag and drop the downloaded file into the Terminal window (its path fills in automatically).
  5. Press Enter and follow the on-screen prompts, then drag Rec onto Applications.
user@macbook ~ % sh ~/Downloads/install-rec.command
▸ Checking for build tools…

First time only: the installer may ask to install Apple's Command Line Tools (a small official Apple download). This lets your Mac build the app locally — which is why macOS trusts it, with no "damaged app" warnings.
